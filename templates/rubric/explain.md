# Rubric: explain (teach-back)

Score each dimension 0-20; total 0-100. Pass at >= 70.

**Before scoring — try to refute.** Read the answer as a skeptic: hunt for one factual error, one hand-wave, one missing step. Only score what survives that pass. The model defaults to agreeing with the learner; this step is the counterweight. When torn between two bands, pick the lower.

**Band, every dimension:** `0-5` absent or wrong · `6-12` partial, real gaps · `13-16` solid, minor gaps · `17-20` full (column below).

| Dimension | Full marks (17-20) | Zero marks (0-5) |
|-----------|--------------------|------------------|
| Correctness | No factual errors or misconceptions. | Core claims are wrong. |
| Mechanism | Explains WHY/HOW it works, not just what it is. | Restates the definition; no mechanism. |
| Clarity | A novice would follow it; every term is defined. | Jargon-soup; a novice is lost. |
| Completeness | Covers the core of the focus, no major gaps. | Misses the central idea. |
| Independence | Done from memory, in the learner's own words. | Paraphrased from open notes. |

`evaluate` records: total score, pass/fail, and the single weakest dimension to work on next.
